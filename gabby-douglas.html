Surviving the Unthinkable

In 2017, Gabby Douglas revealed something that shook the gymnastics world to its core. She had been sexually abused by Dr. Larry Nassar, the USA Gymnastics team doctor.

Coming forward was terrifying. Gabby knew she would face scrutiny, disbelief, and judgment. But she also knew that staying silent would allow the abuse to continue.

Her testimony helped bring Nassar to justice. He was sentenced to hundreds of years in prison. And Gabby, along with dozens of other survivors, became an advocate for protecting young athletes from abuse.

The experience added another layer of pain to Gabby's story. She had already endured poverty, homesickness, bullying, and racism. Now she carried the trauma of abuse.

But she refused to be defined by it. She sought therapy. She spoke out. She used her platform to push for systemic change in gymnastics.
