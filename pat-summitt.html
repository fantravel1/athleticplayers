Building Women's Basketball

When Pat Summitt started coaching in 1974, women's basketball was an afterthought. Games were played in empty gyms. There were no scholarships. No television coverage. No respect.

Pat changed all of that.

She demanded equal treatment for her team. She fought for better facilities, better funding, better opportunities. She refused to accept that women's sports should be second-class.

Under her leadership, Tennessee women's basketball became a powerhouse. Games moved from small gyms to packed arenas. Television networks started broadcasting games. Corporate sponsors took notice.

Pat's success forced the sports world to pay attention to women's basketball. She proved that if you gave women's sports resources and respect, people would watch. Fans would show up. Revenue would follow.

By the time she retired, Tennessee women's basketball was selling out 20,000-seat arenas. The program generated millions in revenue. And women's basketball had become a major sport.

Pat didn't just build a basketball program. She built a movement.
