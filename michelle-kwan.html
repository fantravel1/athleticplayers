Michelle Kwan had four years to process the Nagano loss. Four years to train. Four years to prepare for redemption. The 2002 Salt Lake City Olympics would be her chance to finally win the gold that had eluded her.

The pressure was enormous. The entire nation expected her to win. Her parents, who had sacrificed everything, expected her to win. She expected herself to win.

In the free skate, Michelle skated beautifully‚Äîbut conservatively. She played it safe. She didn't take risks. She skated not to lose rather than to win.
